# Product

## Register

product

## Users

BudgetMate is for people and households who want a practical, local-first way to track monthly spending, shared expenses, recurring bills, and settlement balances without managing a spreadsheet. Users may be checking the app quickly on a phone while shopping, paying bills, reviewing a monthly budget, or settling up with another household member.

## Product Purpose

BudgetMate helps users understand where their money is going, stay aware of monthly pacing, manage category budgets, and make shared-budget responsibility clear. Success means a user can open the app, trust the numbers, understand the current state of their budget, and take the next action without hunting through settings or decoding finance jargon.

## Brand Personality

Calm, trustworthy, clear.

The product should feel like a steady everyday finance companion: approachable enough for shared households, precise enough for money, and quiet enough to use repeatedly. It should reduce anxiety around budgeting rather than dramatize it.

## Anti-references

Do not make BudgetMate feel childish, overly cute, or novelty-themed. Avoid an overly beige, craft-paper look; avoid sterile corporate-bank UI; avoid crypto or finance-bro visual language; avoid loud gradients, glassmorphism, neon accents, and excessive color. The interface should not bury important money states in decorative cards or make Settings feel like a miscellaneous dump.

## Design Principles

1. Lead with financial clarity. The most important amounts, pacing, and balance states should be instantly scannable.
2. Keep trust visible. Sync, shared-budget membership, and settlement states should use plain language and clear state indicators.
3. Use warmth with restraint. BudgetMate can feel human and friendly, but the product should stay practical and mature.
4. Make shared money understandable. Member filters, split expenses, invitations, and settlement breakdowns should show who is involved and why.
5. Prefer familiar controls. Standard SwiftUI patterns, accessible labels, and consistent button/list/card vocabulary should beat novelty.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast where applicable. Design for Dynamic Type-friendly SwiftUI layouts, color-blind-safe status and category cues, reduced-motion-friendly interactions, and clear non-color indicators for important states such as income, expense, warning, sync issue, invite pending, paid, and owed.
